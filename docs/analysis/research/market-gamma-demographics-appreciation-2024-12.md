## Executive Summary

### Key Findings

1. **Bedroom Distribution (MKT-05):**
   - 3-bedroom homes dominate the market at approximately 47% of new construction nationally
   - 4-bedroom homes represent approximately 32-33% of new construction
   - The typical purchased home has 3 bedrooms and 2 bathrooms (consistent 2020-2024)
   - Requiring 4+ bedrooms is **not** overly restrictive for the Phoenix market, particularly given multigenerational housing trends (17% of buyers in 2024)
   - 4-bedroom demand is supported by: larger millennial family sizes, multigenerational living (all-time high), and "forever home" purchasing patterns

2. **Appreciation Analysis (MKT-07):**
   - Phoenix metro appreciated 54.6% from January 2020 ($293K) to January 2025 ($453K)
   - Top appreciating areas: Desert Ridge (+27.2% 2022-2023), Maricopa (68.8% 5-year), Buckeye (69.9% 5-year), Tolleson (74.5% 5-year)
   - Underperforming areas: West Valley and Pinal County cities shifted to buyer's markets in 2024; 86.9% of Phoenix homes lost value Nov 2024-Oct 2025
   - Key appreciation drivers: School district quality, employment growth, infrastructure/transit access, population influx

3. **Recommendations for PHX Houses Filtering:**
   - **4-bedroom requirement is appropriate** given multigenerational trends and millennial family preferences
   - **Emerging neighborhoods to watch:** Laveen, Buckeye, Surprise, Maricopa, Queen Creek
   - **Appreciation risk areas:** Surprise, Goodyear, Cave Creek (shifted to buyer's markets)

### Multigenerational Housing Trend (Critical for 4-Bedroom Demand) [High Confidence]

**2024 Statistics:**
- **17% of all home buyers** purchased multigenerational homes (all-time high, up from 14% prior year)
- **1 in 4 homeowners** now live in multigenerational households
- **Gen X** (ages 45-60) most likely to live in multigenerational households (~20%)

**Reasons for Multigenerational Purchases (2024 vs 2015):**
| Reason | 2024 | 2015 |
|--------|------|------|
| Cost savings | 36% | 15% |
| Adult children moving back | 21% | 11% |
| Care for aging parents | 25% | N/A |
| Adult children never left | 20% | 7% |

**Implication for 4-Bedroom Requirement:** The surge in multigenerational living strongly supports requiring 4+ bedrooms, as these households need additional space for multiple generations.

**Sources:**
- [NAR Multigenerational Housing Report](https://www.nar.realtor/blogs/economists-outlook/one-big-happy-household-how-families-and-the-data-are-shaping-multigenerational-living)
- [Federal Reserve Bank of Boston](https://www.bostonfed.org/publications/current-policy-perspectives/2025/housing-trends-older-households-moving-less-multigenerational-living-rising.aspx)
- [Good Housekeeping - Multigenerational Homes](https://www.goodhousekeeping.com/home/a65050025/multi-generational-homes-are-on-the-rise/)

### Is Requiring 4+ Bedrooms Overly Restrictive? [Medium Confidence]

**Analysis:**

| Factor | Finding | Impact on 4-Bed Requirement |
|--------|---------|----------------------------|
| Market share | 32.4% of new construction | Moderate inventory (~1/3 of market) |
| Buyer demand | Rising (multigenerational, larger families) | Supports requirement |
| Price premium | 4-bed homes ~25% higher median price | Affordability consideration |
| Phoenix household size | 2.51 average (AZ) | Larger than national (supports 4-bed) |
| Forever home trend | 40% of millennials plan to stay 16+ years | Supports buying larger |

**Conclusion:** Requiring 4+ bedrooms is **NOT overly restrictive** for the Phoenix market because:
1. Phoenix has larger household sizes than the national average
2. Multigenerational housing is at an all-time high (17%)
3. Millennials are buying larger homes as "forever homes" rather than starter homes
4. Nearly 40% of 25-44 year old buyers plan to stay 16+ years
5. Phoenix's growth market has substantial 4-bedroom inventory

**However**, buyers should expect:
- Longer search times (moderate inventory)
- Premium pricing (~25% above market median)
- Potential competition for quality 4-bedroom listings

**Source:** [Real Estate Agent PDX - Bedroom Price Analysis](https://realestateagentpdx.com/how-much-more-does-a-4-bedroom-home-sell-for-than-a-3-bedroom-home/36398)

### Phoenix-Specific Data [Medium Confidence]

**Average Phoenix Single-Family Home:**
- 2.93 beds and 2.32 baths
- Average list price: $483,450

This suggests Phoenix skews toward smaller homes on average, making 4-bedroom properties somewhat more specialized but not rare.
